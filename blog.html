Top 5 Tips for Natural Light Portraits

Published on April 15, 2025

Natural light is a photographer’s best friend — when used right. The warmth, the softness, and the depth it adds to portraits can’t be matched by artificial lighting. Here are five tips to help you master it:

1. **Golden Hour is Gold** – Shoot during early morning or late afternoon for that golden, diffused glow.
2. **Use Reflectors** – If shadows are too harsh, use a reflector to bounce light back onto your subject.
3. **Find Open Shade** – Overhead sunlight can be unflattering. Look for shaded areas that still receive ambient light.
4. **Backlighting for Mood** – Position your subject with the sun behind them for a dreamy, halo-lit effect.
5. **Watch the Eyes** – Natural light should bring out catchlights in the subject’s eyes — this is where emotion shines through.

Experiment, observe, and practice — every lighting situation teaches you something new!

Behind the Scenes: Wedding in Tuscany

Published on May 25, 2025

Last month, I had the joy of capturing a dreamy wedding set among the vineyards of Tuscany. From golden sunset shots to candlelit dinner moments, every frame told a love story.

The ceremony took place in a 15th-century villa surrounded by olive trees and rolling hills. The couple wanted a natural, documentary style — so I focused on raw emotion and spontaneous laughter, rather than posed portraits.

One of my favorite moments? The groom wiping a tear as the bride walked down the aisle, backlit by the soft Italian sun. Magic.
